Lake Tamblingan: A Serene Highland Retreat

Munduk Village, Buleleng District, Buleleng Regency

4.5 (220+ Reviews)

Open At 9.00 AM - 5.00 PM Local Time

About

Nestled in the highlands of Bali, Lake Tamblingan is a serene and picturesque lake surrounded by lush forests and misty mountains. Located in the northern part of the island, near Munduk village in Buleleng, this lake is a hidden gem, offering a peaceful retreat away from the bustling tourist spots. It’s an ideal destination for nature lovers and those seeking tranquility.

Lake Tamblingan is known for its pristine waters and the traditional Balinese temples that dot its shores. The lake, along with its twin lake Lake Buyan, forms part of a caldera and is a significant source of fresh water for the surrounding communities. The serene atmosphere and the natural beauty of the area make it a perfect spot for relaxation and reflection.

Highlights

  • Explore the tranquil waters of Lake Tamblingan by traditional canoe
  • Visit the ancient temples around the lake, rich in history and culture
  • Enjoy the scenic views and take stunning photos of the misty highlands

What To Expect

What sets Lake Tamblingan apart from other destinations in Bali is its serene environment and cultural significance:


  • Traditional Canoeing: Experience the tranquility of the lake by paddling a traditional Balinese canoe, known as a "jukung," across its calm waters.
  • Temple Exploration: The shores of Lake Tamblingan are home to several ancient temples, including Pura Ulun Danu Tamblingan, which offer a glimpse into Bali’s rich cultural and spiritual heritage.
  • Nature Walks: The surrounding forests and hills provide excellent opportunities for nature walks and trekking, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the natural beauty of the highlands.

Why Visit Lake Tamblingan?

Whether you’re seeking a peaceful escape into nature or looking to explore Bali’s cultural heritage, Lake Tamblingan offers a unique and serene experience. It’s an off-the-beaten-path destination that allows you to enjoy the island’s natural beauty in a tranquil setting. With its options for canoeing, temple visits, and nature walks, it’s the perfect spot for those looking to unwind and connect with nature.
